Sylhet

Sylhet is one of the oldest cities in Bangladesh having a vast historical and cultural background. It is a city in eastern Bangladesh, on the Surma River. It's known for its Sufi shrines, like the ornate tomb and mosque of 14th-century saint Hazrat Shah Jalal, now a major pilgrimage site near Dargah Gate. The tiny Museum of Rajas contains the belongings of the local folk poet Hasan Raja. A 3-domed gateway stands at the 17th-century Shahi Eidgah, a huge open-air hilltop mosque built by Emperor Aurangzeb. Sylhet tour is your perfect holiday destination during the monsoon and rainy season in Bangladesh. Sylhet is the holy mystical city & experiences waterways. Ratargul Swamp Forest is the only swamp forest in Bangladesh that is situated in Sylhet. Ambil-Jaflong is where you'll witness inspiring views of the surrounding greenery and also get a chance to visit the waterfalls. Do also visit Sree Mangal, considered to be the largest tea garden in the world. Pangthumai Waterfall and Satchari National Park are some of the other places you can visit when you are around Sylhet. If Sylhet is the only place on your itinerary, be aware that only a few hours are sufficient to explore and learn about the place.
